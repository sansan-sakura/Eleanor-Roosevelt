Eleanor Roosevelt

An American political figure, diplomat and activist

Who is Eleanor Roosevelt?

She was the first lady of the United States, wife of Franklin D. Roosevelt, the president of United State from 1933 to 1945. She was a very supportive wife, but also involved numerous humanitarian causes throughout her life. She was one of the most active first lady and changed the role of the first lady. She especially advocated for human rights and worked for political, radical, social justice. After her husband’s death, she became chair of the United Nation’s Human Right Commission. She reminded active in politics and was a prolific writer until her death.
